# Understanding Compound Interest

## The Eighth Wonder of the World

Albert Einstein reportedly called compound interest "the eighth wonder of the world." Whether or not he actually said it, the concept is genuinely powerful for building wealth over time.

## Simple vs. Compound Interest

### Simple Interest
Earned only on your original investment (principal).
- Formula: Interest = Principal × Rate × Time
- Example: $10,000 at 5% simple interest for 10 years = $5,000 in interest

### Compound Interest
Earned on your principal AND on previously earned interest — "interest on interest."
- Formula: A = P(1 + r/n)^(nt)
- Example: $10,000 at 5% compounded annually for 10 years = $6,289 in interest

The $1,289 difference comes from earning interest on your interest!

## The Rule of 72

A quick mental math shortcut: divide 72 by your annual return rate to estimate how many years it takes to double your money.

- At 6% return: 72 ÷ 6 = 12 years to double
- At 8% return: 72 ÷ 8 = 9 years to double
- At 10% return: 72 ÷ 10 = 7.2 years to double

## Why Time Matters Most

The most powerful variable in compound interest is TIME, not the amount you invest.

### Example: Early vs. Late Investor
- **Alex** starts investing $200/month at age 25, stops at 35 (10 years, $24,000 invested)
- **Sam** starts investing $200/month at age 35, continues to 65 (30 years, $72,000 invested)

At 8% average annual return:
- Alex at 65: ~$520,000 (invested only $24,000!)
- Sam at 65: ~$300,000 (invested $72,000)

Alex invested less money but started earlier — and ended up with more.

## Practical Applications

1. **Start investing as early as possible** — even small amounts matter
2. **Reinvest dividends** to maximize compounding
3. **Be patient** — compounding accelerates over decades
4. **Minimize fees** — even 1% in fees significantly reduces compounding over time
5. **Use tax-advantaged accounts** to avoid tax drag on compounding
