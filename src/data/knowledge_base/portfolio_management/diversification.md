# Portfolio Diversification: Why It Matters

## What Is Diversification?

Diversification is the practice of spreading your investments across different asset types, sectors, and geographies to reduce risk. The idea is simple: don't put all your eggs in one basket.

## Why Diversify?

When you concentrate your portfolio in a single stock or sector, your financial health depends entirely on that one investment. If it drops 50%, your entire portfolio drops 50%.

By diversifying, when one investment underperforms, others may compensate. This smooths out your overall returns over time.

## Types of Diversification

### 1. Asset Class Diversification
Spread investments across different asset types:
- **Stocks** (equities) — higher growth potential, higher risk
- **Bonds** (fixed income) — lower returns, more stability
- **Real Estate** (REITs) — income and inflation protection
- **Cash/Money Market** — stability and liquidity

### 2. Sector Diversification
Don't concentrate in one industry:
- Technology, Healthcare, Finance, Consumer, Energy, Utilities, etc.

### 3. Geographic Diversification
Invest in multiple regions:
- Domestic (US) stocks
- International developed markets (Europe, Japan)
- Emerging markets (China, India, Brazil)

### 4. Company Size Diversification
Mix different capitalizations:
- Large-cap (stable, established companies)
- Mid-cap (growth potential with some stability)
- Small-cap (higher growth potential, higher volatility)

## Simple Diversification with Index Funds

The easiest way to achieve diversification is through index funds or ETFs:
- **S&P 500 fund (VOO, SPY)**: 500 largest US companies
- **Total market fund (VTI)**: ~4,000 US stocks
- **International fund (VXUS)**: Global ex-US stocks
- **Bond fund (BND)**: Broad US bond market

A simple "3-fund portfolio" (US stocks + international stocks + bonds) provides excellent diversification.
