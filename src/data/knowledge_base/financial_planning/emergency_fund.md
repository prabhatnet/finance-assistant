# Building an Emergency Fund

## What Is an Emergency Fund?

An emergency fund is money set aside in a safe, easily accessible account to cover unexpected expenses — job loss, medical bills, car repairs, or home emergencies.

## Why It's Your First Financial Priority

Before investing in the stock market, most financial experts recommend building an emergency fund. Without one, you may be forced to sell investments at a loss or take on high-interest debt to cover unexpected costs.

## How Much Do You Need?

### General Guidelines
- **Starter goal**: $1,000 (covers small emergencies)
- **Standard recommendation**: 3-6 months of essential expenses
- **Conservative approach**: 6-12 months (for variable income or single-income households)

### Calculate Your Target
Add up your monthly essential expenses:
- Housing (rent/mortgage)
- Utilities
- Food
- Insurance
- Transportation
- Minimum debt payments

Multiply by your target number of months.

## Where to Keep It

Your emergency fund should be:
- **Safe**: No risk of losing principal
- **Liquid**: Accessible within 1-2 business days
- **Earning some interest**: Beat inflation if possible

Best options:
1. **High-Yield Savings Account (HYSA)** — Currently 4-5% APY
2. **Money Market Account** — Similar rates with check-writing ability
3. **Short-term Treasury bills** — Government-backed, very safe

NOT recommended: stocks, crypto, CDs with penalties, or cash under the mattress.

## How to Build It

1. **Start small**: Even $25/week adds up to $1,300/year
2. **Automate**: Set up automatic transfers on payday
3. **Use windfalls**: Tax refunds, bonuses, gifts
4. **Cut one expense**: Redirect savings to your emergency fund
5. **Don't touch it**: Only for true emergencies
