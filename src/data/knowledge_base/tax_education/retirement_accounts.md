# Tax-Advantaged Retirement Accounts

## Overview

Tax-advantaged accounts let your investments grow faster by reducing or eliminating taxes on your gains. Understanding these accounts is one of the most impactful financial decisions you can make.

## Traditional 401(k)

### How It Works
- Contributions are made pre-tax (reduces your taxable income today)
- Investments grow tax-deferred (no taxes on gains while in the account)
- Withdrawals in retirement are taxed as ordinary income

### Key Details (2024)
- Contribution limit: $23,000/year ($30,500 if age 50+)
- Employer match: Free money! Always contribute enough to get the full match
- Required Minimum Distributions (RMDs) start at age 73

### Best For
People who expect to be in a lower tax bracket in retirement

## Roth IRA

### How It Works
- Contributions are made with after-tax money (no tax deduction today)
- Investments grow completely tax-free
- Withdrawals in retirement are 100% tax-free

### Key Details (2024)
- Contribution limit: $7,000/year ($8,000 if age 50+)
- Income limits apply for direct contributions
- No Required Minimum Distributions (RMDs)
- Contributions (not gains) can be withdrawn anytime without penalty

### Best For
- Young investors expecting higher income later
- Anyone who wants tax-free income in retirement
- Those who want flexibility to access contributions early

## Traditional IRA

### How It Works
- Similar to Traditional 401(k) but not employer-sponsored
- Contributions may be tax-deductible (depends on income and workplace plan)
- Tax-deferred growth, taxed on withdrawal

### Key Details (2024)
- Contribution limit: $7,000/year ($8,000 if age 50+)
- Deductibility phases out at certain income levels if you have a workplace plan
- RMDs start at age 73

## Health Savings Account (HSA) — The Triple Tax Advantage

### How It Works
- Contributions are tax-deductible
- Growth is tax-free
- Withdrawals for medical expenses are tax-free

### Key Details (2024)
- Requires a High-Deductible Health Plan (HDHP)
- Contribution limit: $4,150 individual / $8,300 family
- After age 65, can withdraw for any purpose (taxed as income, like a Traditional IRA)

### Why It's Special
The only account with THREE tax benefits. Many experts consider it the best retirement vehicle available.

## Priority Order for Contributions

1. 401(k) up to employer match (free money)
2. Max out HSA (if eligible)
3. Max out Roth IRA
4. Max out 401(k) to full limit
5. Taxable brokerage account
