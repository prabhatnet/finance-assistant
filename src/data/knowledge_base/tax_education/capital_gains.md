# Capital Gains Tax: What Investors Need to Know

## What Are Capital Gains?

A capital gain occurs when you sell an investment for more than you paid for it. The profit is subject to capital gains tax.

- **Capital Gain** = Selling Price - Purchase Price (Cost Basis)
- Example: Buy stock at $50, sell at $75 → $25 capital gain per share

## Short-Term vs. Long-Term Capital Gains

The tax rate depends on how long you held the investment:

### Short-Term Capital Gains (held < 1 year)
- Taxed as **ordinary income** (same rate as your salary)
- Rates range from 10% to 37% depending on your tax bracket
- This is why day trading can be tax-inefficient

### Long-Term Capital Gains (held ≥ 1 year)
- **Preferential tax rates**: 0%, 15%, or 20%
- Most people pay 15%
- 0% rate applies to lower income levels
- 20% rate for very high earners

### Key Takeaway
Holding investments for at least one year can significantly reduce your tax bill.

## Tax-Loss Harvesting

You can use investment losses to offset gains:
- Losses first offset gains of the same type (short-term offsets short-term)
- Remaining losses offset the other type
- Up to $3,000 of net losses can offset ordinary income per year
- Excess losses carry forward to future years

### Wash Sale Rule
You cannot claim a loss if you buy a "substantially identical" security within 30 days before or after the sale. This prevents selling for a tax loss and immediately rebuying.

## Dividends and Taxes

### Qualified Dividends
- Taxed at long-term capital gains rates (0%, 15%, 20%)
- Must hold the stock for 60+ days around the ex-dividend date

### Ordinary (Non-Qualified) Dividends
- Taxed as ordinary income
- Includes most REIT dividends and short-term holding dividends

## Tax-Efficient Investing Strategies

1. **Hold investments long-term** to qualify for lower rates
2. **Use tax-advantaged accounts** (IRA, 401k) for tax-inefficient investments
3. **Place bonds and REITs in tax-deferred accounts** (they generate ordinary income)
4. **Keep index funds in taxable accounts** (they're naturally tax-efficient)
5. **Harvest losses** strategically to offset gains
